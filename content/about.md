# About Fellowship of the Flux

## Our Story

Fellowship of the Flux began in Kansas City with a simple idea: everyone should have the opportunity to learn soldering skills in a supportive, hands-on environment. What started as volunteer-run workshops at local cybersecurity conferences like BSides KC and MakerFaire KC has grown into a regional resource for STEM education.

Our name playfully references the Fellowship of the Ring, emphasizing our belief that learning is a shared journey. Just as flux enables solder to flow and create strong connections, we help knowledge flow between experienced makers and eager learners, creating lasting connections in our community.

## Our Mission

**Advancing the art of learning to solder** through community outreach, education, and resource sharing.

## What We Do

### Community Workshops
We provide hands-on soldering workshops at:
- Cybersecurity conferences (BSides KC, regional BSides events)
- Maker faires and maker spaces
- STEM education events
- School and library programs
- Corporate team-building events

### Portable Soldering Labs
Our carefully designed portable labs feature:
- 20+ Hakko FX-888 temperature-controlled soldering stations
- Custom 3D-printed organizers for tools and components
- Complete sets of essential tools (flush cutters, solder dispensers, desoldering equipment)
- Safety equipment and workspace materials
- Beginner-friendly project kits

### Educational Resources
We develop and share:
- Step-by-step soldering tutorials
- Equipment selection guides
- Safety protocols and best practices
- Curriculum materials for educators
- Lab setup and organization guides

### Community Building
We foster learning communities by:
- Training volunteers to lead workshops
- Connecting organizations with resources
- Sharing knowledge and best practices
- Supporting other maker education initiatives

## Our Impact

Since our founding, Fellowship of the Flux has:

- **Taught 500+ people** to solder at community events
- **Supported 25+ conferences** and educational events
- **Trained 30+ volunteers** to lead their own workshops
- **Reached learners** from elementary school through retirement
- **Expanded across the Midwest** with partner organizations

## Our Values

### Inclusivity
Soldering education should be accessible to everyone, regardless of background, age, or prior experience.

### Safety First
We prioritize safe practices and create environments where learners feel secure to experiment and make mistakes.

### Community Over Competition
We believe in collaboration, knowledge sharing, and lifting each other up.

### Quality Education
We use professional-grade equipment and proven teaching methods to ensure learners develop real skills.

### Volunteer Spirit
Our work is driven by passion for education and community service, not profit.
