# Fellowship of the Flux - Brand Guidelines

## Mission Statement

Fellowship of the Flux is a Kansas City-based volunteer organization dedicated to advancing the art of learning to solder through community outreach, education, and resource sharing. We provide portable soldering labs, educational workshops, and resources to help individuals and organizations build soldering skills and create their own educational programs.

## Brand Story

Born from the maker and cybersecurity communities in Kansas City, Fellowship of the Flux began by providing "Learn to Solder" workshops at community events like MakerFaire KC and BSides KC. Our name playfully references the Fellowship of the Ring, emphasizing our community-driven mission to share knowledge and skills. We've grown from a local initiative to support STEM education across the Midwest, providing both direct services and resources for others to replicate our model.

## Target Audiences

### Primary Audiences
1. **Event Organizers** - Conference organizers, maker spaces, and community groups seeking soldering workshops
2. **Educators & Volunteers** - Teachers, workshop leaders, and volunteers wanting to run soldering education programs
3. **Individual Learners** - People wanting to learn soldering skills for personal projects or career development
4. **Organizations** - Schools, libraries, and community groups looking to build their own soldering education programs

### Secondary Audiences
- **Sponsors & Supporters** - Companies and individuals who want to support STEM education
- **Equipment Vendors** - Partners in the soldering and electronics education space
- **Other Maker Communities** - Groups looking to collaborate or share resources

## Brand Personality

### Core Traits
- **Welcoming & Inclusive** - Everyone can learn, regardless of background or experience level
- **Educational & Approachable** - Technical expertise presented in accessible ways
- **Community-Focused** - Collaboration and shared learning over individual achievement
- **Professional & Trustworthy** - Reliable partner for organizations and events
- **Passionate & Dedicated** - Genuine enthusiasm for the craft and teaching

### Tone of Voice
- **Encouraging** - "You can do this" attitude
- **Clear & Practical** - Straightforward instructions and explanations
- **Collaborative** - "We're in this together" approach
- **Respectful** - Acknowledging different skill levels and learning styles
- **Enthusiastic** - Genuine excitement about soldering and making

### Typography Guidelines

#### Primary Font Family
- **Headings**: Inter (Modern, clean, professional)
- **Body Text**: Inter (Consistent, highly readable)
- **Code/Technical**: JetBrains Mono (Monospace for technical content)

#### Font Usage
- **H1**: Bold, 2.5rem, used for page titles and major sections
- **H2**: Semibold, 2rem, used for section headers
- **H3**: Semibold, 1.5rem, used for subsections
- **Body**: Regular, 1rem, primary text content
- **Caption**: Regular, 0.875rem, image captions and metadata

### Imagery Style

#### Photography Guidelines
- **Workshop Scenes**: People collaborating, hands-on learning, diverse participants
- **Equipment Shots**: Clean, well-lit photos of tools and setups
- **Process Documentation**: Step-by-step photos showing techniques
- **Community Events**: Candid shots of workshops, participants engaged in learning
- **Before/After**: Progress shots showing learning outcomes

#### Illustration Style
- **Technical Diagrams**: Clean, precise, educational
- **Icons**: Simple, consistent, functional
- **Infographics**: Clear data visualization, process flows
- **Badge/Achievement Graphics**: Modern, professional skill indicators

### Logo Usage

#### Primary Logo Elements
- Fellowship of the Flux wordmark
- Soldering iron icon (when standalone icon needed)
- Combined logo + tagline for formal usage

#### Logo Guidelines
- Maintain clear space equal to the height of the "F" in Fellowship
- Use on light backgrounds primarily
- Dark version available for light backgrounds
- Single color versions for special applications

## Content Strategy

### Editorial Guidelines

#### Content Principles
1. **Accessibility First** - Content should be understandable by beginners
2. **Safety Emphasis** - Always prioritize safe practices and procedures
3. **Community Focus** - Highlight collaboration and shared learning
4. **Practical Application** - Provide actionable, useful information
5. **Inclusive Language** - Welcome all backgrounds and skill levels

#### Content Types
- **Educational Tutorials** - Step-by-step learning content
- **Resource Guides** - Equipment recommendations, setup guides
- **Community Stories** - Event recaps, success stories, volunteer spotlights
- **Technical References** - In-depth guides for advanced topics
- **Organization Resources** - Materials for event planners and educators

## Implementation Guidelines

### Website Application

### Social Media
- Share workshop photos and success stories

## Brand Applications

### Do's
✅ Use approved color combinations
✅ Maintain consistent typography
✅ Show diverse, inclusive workshop participants
✅ Emphasize safety and best practices
✅ Highlight community and collaboration
✅ Use clear, jargon-free language when possible

### Don'ts
❌ Use colors outside the approved palette
❌ Mix too many font families
❌ Show unsafe soldering practices
❌ Use overly technical language without explanation
❌ Focus solely on individual achievement
❌ Ignore accessibility considerations
